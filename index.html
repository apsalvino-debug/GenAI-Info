FAQs

What is Generative AI?
Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence that creates new content instead of just analyzing data. It learns from existing information—both obvious and hidden patterns—and can produce text, images, music, code, or videos.
How does Generative AI work?
Generative AI learns from large datasets, studying patterns and structures. It predicts what comes next to generate new content. Think of it like a smart chef: it studies thousands of recipes and creates unique dishes based on them.
Examples of Generative AI tools?
Tools include ChatGPT, DALL·E, MidJourney, and others that generate text, images, or music.
Limitations
- Produces inaccurate information.
- Can hallucinate facts.
- Limited by training data.
- Cannot fully understand context or emotions.
- May struggle with complex reasoning.
- No real-time access unless updated.
Risks
- May amplify biases.
- Risk of misinformation.
- Can be used for deepfakes or harmful content.
- Potential privacy issues.
- May reduce certain human jobs if mismanaged.

Ethical Use of AI

Responsible use in Academe
AI should support learning, not replace it. Students can use AI to clarify concepts, brainstorm, or check understanding—but genuine effort must remain central.
Avoiding plagiarism and misuse
AI-generated content should not be copied as original work. Misusing AI to cheat or fabricate data is academic misconduct. AI should assist, not replace genuine effort.
Accuracy and verification
AI may generate incorrect or outdated information. Always verify facts and compare with reliable references. Treat AI responses as suggestions.
Bias and fairness
AI models may produce biased content. Ethical AI use means questioning outputs, avoiding spread of biased info, and evaluating responses carefully.

Analogy on the Use of AI in Dev Work

Hiring a wedding team using an iPhone 16 Pro Max and CapCut for ₱40k might sound simple, but you’re really paying for their skill, experience, and storytelling, not just the tools. The equipment is accessible—what matters is how well the team uses it to deliver quality results.

How does this relate to AI?
AI tools are the same. Anyone can generate code, but only real developers understand how to shape it, fix issues, and build something reliable.
Key takeaway for developers
Generative AI can speed up tasks and inspire ideas, but quality software depends on skill, integrity, and thoughtful problem-solving—not just copying AI output.
